Uppu Chaapa

"Salted fish", holds a significant legacy within Andhra cuisine and beyond. It's a traditional method of preserving and preparing fish, particularly in coastal Andhra and Rayalaseema regions. The fish, dried and salted, is a flavorful ingredient in various dishes like curries, fries, and masalas

Guttu-Vankaaya

Gutti Vankaya is a curried dish of stuffed eggplants from Andhra and Telangana cuisines. Small young brinjals are stuffed with a spicy ground peanut masala and simmered with onions, tomatoes, spices and curry leaves.

Gongura Pachdi

Gongura, a leafy green also known as sorrel or rosella, has a legacy rooted in traditional Indian cuisine and medicinal practices. Its unique flavor and nutritional value make it a popular ingredient in Andhra Pradesh and South India, while its potential for global appeal continues to grow
